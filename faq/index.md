    <div class="faq-list reveal">
      <details class="faq-item" open>
        <summary>What is Panta Rhei?</summary>
        <div><p>Panta Rhei is a seven-volume research program exploring whether mathematics—and much of what we call reality—can be rebuilt from structure alone, using a categorical vocabulary as the primary scaffold. Each volume can be read independently, but together they form a single arc from foundations to holomorphy and spectrum, then into physics, life, and metaphysics.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Is this a textbook, a monograph, or a research manifesto?</summary>
        <div><p>It is closest to a research program presented in book form. Some parts aim for theorem-style development inside the framework; other parts are explicitly programmatic and marked as such. The intent is to be precise about scope, mechanisms, and what is established versus conjectural.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Who is the intended audience?</summary>
        <div><p>Mathematicians, theoretical computer scientists, physicists, philosophers, and researchers working at the interfaces of structure, meaning, mind, ethics, and social systems. The series is written to be readable in layers—high level first, then deeper technical detail.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Where should I start reading?</summary>
        <div><p>Two good entry points are Book I (<em>Categorical Foundations</em>) if you want the axioms and core scaffolding, or Book III (<em>Categorical Spectrum</em>) if you want a guided map of the big themes first. Canonical order is Book I → VII, but each volume is designed to stand on its own.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>What do τ, τ³, and the lemniscate boundary mean?</summary>
        <div><p>Very briefly: τ is the foundational categorical structure introduced in Book I. τ³ = τ¹ ×₍f₎ τ² is a canonical arena used in later volumes. The lemniscate S¹ ∨ S¹ is a boundary/interface motif that organizes several key constructions, including the bulk–boundary correspondence in Book II.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Are the books peer-reviewed?</summary>
        <div><p>The series is published independently. The authors explicitly welcome critical engagement, error-finding, and debate. Where statements are theorems in the internal framework, the aim is to make mechanisms and assumptions explicit; where statements are programmatic, they are labeled accordingly.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Do the books have DOIs, and how should I cite them?</summary>
        <div><p>Yes — each volume is published with a DOI for citation. You can cite the book like a standard monograph and include the DOI. The Citation page centralizes the series’ reference layer.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>Are there supporting materials?</summary>
        <div><p>Yes. Supporting materials—including TOCs, Q&amp;A appendices, reader packs, and code / Lean companions where available—are maintained online. See the Resources page, Press Kit page, and Updates page for the current canonical links.</p></div>
      </details>

      <details class="faq-item">
        <summary>How can I contact the authors?</summary>
        <div><p>Use the Contact page or email <a href="mailto:contact@panta-rhei-books.org">contact@panta-rhei-books.org</a>. For academic correspondence, error reports, and collaboration proposals, include the volume name and, if relevant, chapter or section references.</p></div>
      </details>
